ai ethics

4-21-2025

my professional opinion on ai ethics

ai: it's what everybody is talking about now.

from writing a mean essay to making images that child predators will like on facebook, it has boomed in popularity recently through LLMs (large language models) like chatgpt and it's now being integrated into computers, operating systems, web browsers, etc through stuff like copilot, apple intellegence, aria etc
i am going to state my opinions on ai being used for creative stuff like art and writing

writing

ai can write essays, poems, and more based off other writing through human prompts

i'm pretty qualified to talk about this since i write a lot so uhh
the closest ai will get to my writing is me asking stuff like "is it good" and getting advice and stuff
if i gave it a prompt and told it to write my ideas for me and then said that i made it would be dishonest and undignified
it would show that i didn't care enough to actually do it myself and that i'm a liar
it doesn't matter if the idea was original, actually writing it yourself is the proper display of skills like grammar, vocabulary, etc and if that's hard for you i'm sorry but i just think that's how it is
you could use ai as a learning opportunity and ask it to rate and give you advice on your writing
i do that
and it works
and that's creative writing, which is what i mainly do

academic

turning in unedited ai generated writing as your own creation is academic misconduct. most professors and teachers agree with that statement
a lot of them fear students will use ai to complete assignments with zero effort, bypassing the thinking, intellectual struggle, and learning involved in an assignment
and i think that's a fair concern
what i also think is fair is ai as a writing assistant in some cases
like researching when googling won't give you answers or asking about the quality of your writing

art

ai can generate cohesive images based off other ones through human prompts

this is even more controversial i think and i'm not really qualified to speak here
and to me this brings up question along the lines of
what gives art its value?
it's certainly not like how good the art looks to people
stuff like a random old sketch or a literal blank canvas with a funny title can sell for fortunes
i think it could be the message it brings
going back to the blank canvas, if it's title was like "give me the money" it would symbolize how stupid art can get when it comes to value
if someone was like "i put hours into this" and specifically stated what they made wasn't ai when it really was then that would be undignified
but when it's like they had the idea and put it in a prompt i really don't know
i can say it's kinda not ok with writing because proper display of skills and stuff
and with that logic it could be the same here like
the idea is one thing but showing the other skills that go into making art like drawing, coloring, etc is the complete goal
i really don't know, but it could be that art isn't just about having an idea, it's about being able to realize that idea in a meaningful way.
like, are you low on resources? use what you've got. that could mean making an art piece out of like pieces of log. it's still art, and it'll make your art piece way more meaningful as well.

music

ai can generate music based off other music through human prompts

i don't really know what to say here but
is this even worth it? most ai music i've heard is repetitive and just samey
i don't think anyone actually would like get ai music and be like "guys i made this myself isnt it so cool!1!!??"
could it really ever be truly expressive?
people like to say they like music with 'soul.'
ai can't really achieve that, no?
i don't know

programming

ai can write code based off documentation and previously written code through human prompts

i am a bit qualified to talk about this, i program stuff
i think letting ai write some code for you is completely fine
i do it a lot because programming is quite often confusing
when you're programming, you can't really do anything meaningful unless you know what you're doing
so if i ask chatgpt to give me some code i can use i'm going to have to understand what it has just written
otherwise i'll just have a block of code that i don't understand and that's not good when bugs start popping up
so yeah i think it's completely fine because of the nature of programming

general ethics

there are different ethical concerns depending on how ai is used, and i will list some of the biggest ones:

rights, consent, and plagiarism

here's an example:
most ai art models are trained on huge amounts of data that have artwork from human artists, often without asking for permission
if an ai learns by viewing someone's work without credit or compensation it raises questions about exploitation
you could argue it's similar to how humans learn; by observing, imitating, and remixing
but humans have boundaries like copyright laws and ai models don't always respect that
some outputs can mimic existing media so closely they can blur the line between inspiration and copying

job displacement

something many people fear is that ai and machinery will take their jobs
one example is like horses and cars
sure it's not like we would pay horses or anything but like i'm pretty sure we took care of them most of the time and then cars came and horses in their entirety were no longer needed
same thing with ai
people fear their jobs will be taken in the same way
ideally to avoid job displacement we should keep ai as a tool and not a replacement in my opinion

accessibility

ai gives people the ability to express their creative ideas without traditional langauge, artistic, musical, etc training.
people who couldn't do some creative activites before can now
but as i've stated before, that training does tend to matter
if you can make something with knowing how, does it count as art?
you could say yes, because art is just about if not more about ideas as much as technique
but you could also say no, because art is about intentional choices and those require skill.
so i'm not sure where i stand here.
maybe if ai is just a tool and you're using it with your own purpose, knowledge, and vision, you're still the creator
but if it's doing the thinking and creating for you, then i would argue you are not the creator.

conclusion

this stuff is like philosophically challenging to think about
does learning the craft still matter when ai can do it for you?
i think so
but i could just as easily say i don't.
